Steering
The aim of this lesson is to smoothly steer the car through a 90-degree turn at slow speed. The car should be in a safe area, either on or off the road, with enough room to make 90-degree turns at low speed. If you are driving on the road you will need to use the indicators.





Summary

  1. Have both hands lightly gripping the steering wheel at quarter-to-three position
  2. Steer the car through at least five left and right 90-degree turns
  3. Turn the steering wheel back to the centre each time rather than letting it slip back by itself
  4. Use the appropriate steering method for the speed and how tight the turn is
  5. Keep fully in control of the car throughout the sequence of turns
  6. Look ahead in the direction of the turn, not at the ground in front of the car
Parallel parking
Parallel parking can be intimidating. Especially if you’ve seen videos of people doing it horribly, you might be scared to try to parallel park by yourself. But luckily, there are a few steps you can follow that will work like a charm every time. If you’re ready to parallel park like a pro, just follow the below parallel parking tips, practice a few times, and you’ll be confident to parallel park anywhere





Summary

  1. Drive around until you find a spot that looks big enough for your car
  2. Pull up next to the parked car in front of the spot
  3. Check your mirrors
  4. Stop when you can see the right-front part of the rear
  5. Turn your steering wheel all the way left and slowly continue backing up
  6. Stop, then adjust your car
Reverse bay parking
Reverse bay parking (or parking in a bay in reverse gear, 90 degree reverse parking, perpendicular parking) is one of the driving test maneuvers. Learn how to park from Parking Tutorial experts. This short video shows a simple procedure for bay parking between two cars that involves using reference points. According to these points, the driver can turn the steering wheel at the right moment and can easily park the car. This method of parking in a bay is suitable for parking both between two cars as well as between parking lines.





Summary

  1. Select your target bay and check your surrounding, ensuring there’s ample room to reverse into it
  2. Pull the nose of your vehicle forwards at a 45 degree
  3. Check all around you for pedestrians and other vehicles, paying particular attention to your blind spot
  4. When you’re certain it’s safe and your vehicle is clear, begin reversing slowly
  5. Use the parallel lines either side of you to guide yourself into the bay
  6. Straighten your steering to ensure you end up right in the centre of the bay
